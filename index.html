Md Ajwad Akil

I am a PhD student in the Department of Computer Science in Purdue University. I Started my PhD journey in Fall 2023. Currently I am working under supervision of Professor Elisa Bertino. Prior to this, I received my Bachelors (B.sc) from Department of Computer Science and Engineering with Honors from Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology. During my bachelors, I worked in CSE BUET NLP LAB under the supervision of Professor Rifat Shahriyar.

My current research is at the intersection of malware and software security and Generative AI, with a strong focus on LLM. I am intrigued by the challenge of combining classical software security and LLM and generative AI. I am also exploring about LLM security and defense and also ability of code LM to reason and work with systems and low level languages. Besides I am also engaged in static-analysis and vulnerability based research, particularly taint analysis. My research interests can be broadly categorized as:

  • MALWARE AND ADVERSARIAL MACHINE LEARNING
  • APPLICATION OF LLM, CODE LM AND GENERATIVE AI FOR SECURITY
  • STATIC ANALYSIS AND VULNERABILITY BASED RESEARCH
  • SECURITY OF LLMs, AGENTS AND RELATED AI SYSTEMS
  • REASONING ABILITY OF CODE LM AND GENERAL APPLICATION TO SOFTWARE ENGINEERING
